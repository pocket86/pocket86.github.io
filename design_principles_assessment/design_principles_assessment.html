Proximity Alignment Repetition Contrast Typography
Website: www.harrys.com

Harry's web site, both mobile and desktop, have a great use of proximity. There are almost no visual boarders or colors to seperate different sections of the page, or even the pictures and products that are displayed on the page. However, with the use of good proximity and gruoping, it is very easy for your eyes to navigate the page, and even tell what items belong together. Even in the picture that includes the razor handle, blades, and shaving cream - you can clearly see they are together, but seperate and grouped together.

Author - Weston Clark

Website: www.senate.gov

This website uses a great alignment. There are distinct areas in the page, across the top and three separate areas coming down. The text, pictures, link, and paragraphs are contained in these areas and it is all well aligned. It keeps with the image of the United States Senate being organized.

Author - Parker Goldsberry

Website: www.speedhunters.com

In terms of repetition, Speedhunters.com utilizes pictures regarding the articles with the type of heading underneath. For example, The Mustang Gets Refreshed refers to an article about the 2018 mustang. Once you select the image link, the same navigation from the home page is presented at the top of the page. The background color of the homepage, white, presents itself which in turn clarifies that you are on the same webpage!

Author - Aaron Ferguson

Website: www.speedhunters.com

In terms of contrast, Speedhunters.com pulls off the contrast in a way that draws the reader in very easily. The pictures presented stands out against the white background. The Speedhunters logo at the top of the page is white against black. The headings are exact opposite of the Speedhunters logo, black against White

Author - Aaron Ferguson

Website: www.anddroid.com

In this image I see three different uses of typography. The android logo in the top left is smooth and designed to look cool and trustworthy. The main text is in a font that looks comfortable and inviting. And in the purple button the text is in all caps and is in a font is a little more bold, seeming to say “LET’S DO THIS.”

Author - Parker Goldsberry
